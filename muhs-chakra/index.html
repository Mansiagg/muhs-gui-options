The Vision

The Network of Centres of Excellence (CoE) Policy, 2023, issued by the Government of Maharashtra’s Medical Education and Drugs Department (MEDD), aligns with MUHS’s commitment to drive healthcare standards to unparalleled heights across Maharashtra. The policy emphasizes the establishment of a coordinated Hub-and-Spoke Network to promote excellence in medical education, advanced research, and patient care. This initiative aims to position Maharashtra and its affiliated institutions on a global platform, fostering the highest standards of quality, accessibility, and comprehensive care while addressing the state’s unique healthcare challenges

Enable Educational Excellence

Empower Maharashtra to become the premier state for medical education in India

  • Augment capacity and care by creating high-quality medical personnels
  • Strengthen 10+ GMCs1 to feature among India’s Top 50 Medical Institutions; Establish Maharashtra as the preferred destination for aspiring medical students nationwide
  • Continuous skilling of all medical personnel, to ensure up-to-date practices
Enhance Quality and Affordability of care

Improve health outcomes by guiding high quality service delivery

  • Transformations in health system design (e.g., care delivery/financing) and service delivery models (e.g., population management)
  • High-quality secondary and tertiary care in Maharashtra GMCs
Advance Medical Research

Establish Maharashtra as a hub for clinical research in India and globally

  • Help address the state’s biggest public health challenges e.g., Tuberculosis,Dengue, NCDs2
  • Develop the CoE3 Network as the preferred choice for industry-led medical research, including Pharma and MedTech collaborations
  • Drive significant growth in state-led publications, focusing on priority areas like vaccines, therapeutics, and MedTech advancements

The MUHS COE Model

The MUHS CoE model operates as a Hub-and-Spoke model, with MUHS as the Hub/Apex Centre of Excellence (ACOE). The Spokes/COEs include designated Government Medical Colleges (GMCs) and research labs under MUHS, which will spearhead research and healthcare interventions tailored to regional needs. Each Spoke will focus on topics specific to the demography, terrain, climate, and living conditions of the dependent patient clientele. This network aims to create an interlinked ecosystem where education, research, and clinical care are seamlessly integrated, ensuring equitable healthcare advancements across the state.

6 High Impact Initiatives

The recently constituted Centre of Excellence (CHAKRA) is a transformative initiative under MUHS’s Network of Centres of Excellence (CoE) Policy. CHAKRA, envisioned as a Section 8 Company in the making, has been approved in principle by the Government of Maharashtra through a Government Resolution (GR) dated 9th October 2024. This dynamic initiative focuses on creating capital assets, fostering innovation, and enhancing institutional effectiveness to address Maharashtra’s healthcare, medical education, and research challenges.

Faculty Development Academy

The academy will offer standardized, high-quality courses for clinical, managerial, teaching, and IT domains. This initiative is crucial for institutional effectiveness, enhancing faculty engagement and teaching-learning outcomes.

Disha(Department of Innovations and Systems for Healthcare Associations) Incubation Center

The DISHA Incubation Center, a registered Section 8 Company, will provide healthcare startups with mentorship, funding, and access to infrastructure. It will foster healthcare innovations and entrepreneurial solutions.

IKSHANA(Integrated Knowledge for Sustainable Health Awareness)- Museum

The IKSHANA Health Science Museum will serve as an interactive educational hub, integrating modern and traditional healthcare knowledge. It will promote public health literacy and create awareness about health and well-being.

Simulation Lab

Designed to bridge the gap between theory and practice, the Simulation Lab will provide AR/ VR-enabled simulations and real-time medical scenarios such as emergency care, trauma management, and neonatal care.

Drishti(Division of Research in Interdisciplinary Sciences , Healthcare & Translational Innovations)-Clinical trials Unit

DRISHTI will develop standardized protocols and practices for clinical trials, ensuring efficiency, compliance, and impactful research outcomes. This initiative will streamline statewide clinical research efforts.

Digital Health Department and Digital Learning Studio

The Digital Health Department will facilitate training and research on digital health innovations such as telemedicine, AI for diagnosis, and wearable devices. The Digital Learning Studio will provide live online sessions and self-paced e-learning modules to enhance accessibility and modernize medical education delivery.

Stakeholder Benefits

The MUHS CoE network is envisioned as a transformative initiative aimed at elevating healthcare education, research, and care delivery across Maharashtra. By addressing critical challenges and fostering collaboration, it is designed to ensure that all stakeholders—institutions, faculty, students, patients, and startups—realize significant and measurable benefits.

Benefits to MUHS and Affiliated Colleges

  • Enhanced institutional reputation and global recognition.
  • Increased faculty accountability, research output, and improved student performance.

Benefits to Patients

  • Improved access to affordable and high-quality care with better health outcomes.

Benefits to faculty and Researchers

  • Increased funding opportunities and access to collaborative research.
  • Support for clinical trials, ethical clearances, and access to mentorship networks.

Benefits to Healthcare StartUps

  • Access to funding, infrastructure, and mentorship through the DISHA Incubation Center.

Benefits to Students

  • High-quality training through simulation labs and digital tools.
  • Opportunities for networking and exposure to emerging healthcare startups via incubation programs.
